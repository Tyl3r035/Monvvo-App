How to Use Monvvo Student Loan Calculator

  1. Enter the Loan Amount.
    Provide the total sum of money you need to borrow for your education. This amount is necessary for the student loan calculator to determine your monthly payments.
  2. Input the Annual Interest Rate.
    Specify the annual interest rate for the loan. This rate influences the total interest you’ll pay over the loan term and is crucial for accurate calculations with the student loan calculator payment.
  3. Specify the Loan Term.
    Indicate the length of time over which you will repay the loan, expressed in months. This helps the student loan calculator estimate your monthly payment amount.
  4. Enter the Grace Period.
    Provide the duration of any grace period you have after graduation. This period can delay the start of your repayments, and the student loan calculator will adjust your payment schedule accordingly.
  5. Include the Deferment Period.
    If you are eligible for a deferment period, input the duration here. This helps the student loan calculator provide a more accurate payment schedule by including these pauses.
  6. Add Your Monthly Payment.
    Specify the amount you plan to pay each month towards your loan. This helps the student loan calculator to estimate how your payments will affect the loan term and total interest.
  7. Add Extra Monthly Payment.
    If you plan to make extra monthly payments, input that amount here. Extra payments can reduce the loan term and the total interest paid, which the student loan calculator will account for.
  8. Calculate Your Student Loan Payment.
    Once you have entered all the necessary details, click the “Calculate Student Loan” button to see your estimated monthly payments and total loan cost. This calculation helps you plan your finances effectively.

Student Loan Terms

Loan Amount

Loan amount is the total sum of money you need to borrow for your education. This value is a key input for the student loan calculator.

Annual Interest Rate

Annual interest rate is the percentage charged on the loan amount each year. It is typically expressed as an annual percentage rate (APR) and determines the interest portion of your monthly payments calculated by the student loan calculator payment.

Loan Term

Loan term is the length of time over which you agree to repay your loan. It is expressed in months and affects the monthly payment amount, which is estimated by the student loan calculator.

Grace Period

Grace period is the time after graduation during which you are not required to make loan payments. This period helps you get financially settled before starting repayments.

Deferment Period

Deferment period is a temporary pause in loan payments, often allowed under certain conditions like further education or economic hardship. Including this in the student loan calculator helps provide a more accurate payment schedule.

Monthly Payment

Monthly payment is the amount you plan to pay each month towards your loan. This helps the student loan calculator to estimate how your payments will affect the loan term and total interest.

Extra Monthly Payment

Extra monthly payment is any additional amount you decide to pay each month on top of your regular loan payment. Extra payments can help reduce the loan term and the total interest paid.
