Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

1. How do I use the Monvvo mortgage calculator?

To use the calculator, enter the home price, down payment amount, loan term, and interest rate. You can also include additional expenses like property taxes, PMI, and HOA fees for a more accurate estimate.

2. What is Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI), and when is it required?

PMI is insurance that protects the lender if you default on your mortgage. It's typically required if your down payment is less than 20% of the home's value.

3. Can I use the calculator for refinancing my existing mortgage?

Yes, by inputting your current loan balance, interest rate, and remaining term, you can estimate new payment scenarios.

4. Does the calculator account for property taxes and homeowners insurance?

Yes, you can input estimated amounts for property taxes and homeowners insurance to see how they affect your monthly payment.

5. What is an amortization schedule, and how can I view it?

An amortization schedule shows the breakdown of each payment into principal and interest over the loan term. After calculating, you can view or export this schedule for detailed insights.

6. How does changing the loan term affect my monthly payments?

Shorter loan terms generally result in higher monthly payments but lower total interest paid over the life of the loan. Longer terms have lower monthly payments but higher total interest.

7. Is my data saved when I use the Monvvo calculator?

No, Monvvo does not store any personal data entered into the calculator. All calculations are performed in real-time without saving your information.
