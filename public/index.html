How to Use Monvvo Mortgage Calculator

  1. Input the home’s price.
    Enter the total purchase price of the home you plan to buy or the current market value of your home if you are looking to refinance.
  2. Specify your down payment.
    Enter the amount you intend to pay upfront for the home purchase.
  3. Select the loan duration.
    Choose the term length for your mortgage, commonly 30 years, but you may also consider 20, 15, or even 10 years, depending on your financial strategy.
  4. Provide the interest rate.
    Enter the interest rate you anticipate securing or the rate you are currently paying. The calculator might default to an average rate, but you can customize this to reflect your actual rate.
  5. Enter property taxes.
    Include the annual property tax amount to get a more accurate estimate of your monthly payment. This can usually be found on the property’s listing or by contacting your local tax assessor’s office.
  6. Include PMI.
    If your down payment is less than 20%, you may be required to have private mortgage insurance (PMI). Enter the cost of PMI, which is typically calculated as a percentage of the loan amount.
  7. Add HOA fees.
    If the property is part of a homeowners association, enter the monthly HOA fees. These fees cover the maintenance and amenities provided by the association.
  8. Calculate your Monthly Mortgage Payment.
    Once all the necessary details are filled in, click on the button to calculate your monthly mortgage payment. This will give you an estimate based on the information provided. Click Here to Learn More About Mortgage Calculations.

Mortgage Terms

Home Price

The home price is the total cost of purchasing the property, including the base price of the house and any additional fees or costs associated with the purchase.

Down Payment

The down payment is the initial amount of money you pay upfront when purchasing a home, which is typically a percentage of the total home price. This amount reduces the loan amount you need to borrow.

Loan Term

The loan term is the length of time over which you agree to repay your mortgage loan. It is typically expressed in years, such as 15 or 30 years.

Interest Rate

The interest rate is the percentage charged on the loan amount by the lender, which determines the cost of borrowing money for your mortgage. It is usually expressed as an annual percentage rate (APR).

Property Taxes

Property taxes are annual/monthly taxes paid by homeowners to local government authorities, based on the assessed value of the property. The average property tax rate can be around 1.1% of the property's value.

PMI Expense

Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI) is a type of insurance that protects the lender if you default on your mortgage. It is typically required if your down payment is less than 20% of the home's purchase price.

HOA Fees

Homeowner's Association (HOA) fees are monthly or annual dues paid by homeowners in a residential community to cover the maintenance of common areas and shared amenities.
